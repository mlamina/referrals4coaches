You only pay for real opportunities.

No subscription, no monthly fee. You pay a small flat fee per referral — and only when a new potential client actually reaches out through your link. If nobody bites, you pay nothing. You're never paying for a tool that sits idle, only for real business opportunities, the moment they land.

The mechanics

Why this works.

  • —Warm beats cold, every time. A referral from someone trusted converts where an ad or a cold DM never will.
  • —The referrer does the vouching. Trust is transferred before you ever speak. You start the conversation already credible.
  • —Leads arrive pre-qualified. Your allies only show your QR to people who clearly need what you do.
  • —No ad spend, no funnels. No landing-page tricks, no nurture sequences. Just real introductions in real life.
  • —Your network becomes a channel. The people who already believe in you can finally refer you without it being awkward.
  • —You stay in control. Details come in. You reach out to whoever's a fit. Nobody else gets through.
A note from the founder

I'm Marco, co-founder of JourneyLoop — where we build tools for independent coaches and spend our days listening to them. Coaching can be the loneliest profession in the world; we're trying to make it a little less so.

The same thing comes up in almost every conversation: they're brilliant at the craft, and stuck on getting in front of the right people. Their best clients almost always came from a referral — and almost never from the marketing they agonize over.

The strange part is that referrals are the one channel nobody has made easy. The people who'd happily vouch for you want to send clients your way — they just never have the right thing to hand over at the right moment.

So here's the idea: one simple profile, a personal link your allies keep on your contact card, and a QR they show when they meet someone who needs you. You only hear about it when a real opportunity actually lands.

This started as a side experiment out of the work we do at JourneyLoop. The page you're reading is me testing whether coaches feel this too. If that's you, the next part is a question, not a sign-up.
